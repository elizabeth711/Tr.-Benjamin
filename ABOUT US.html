Our History

Kaliro High School is a Christian founded, government-aided secondary school located in kaliro Town, Busoga sub-region, Eastern Uganda. The school enrolls the students for both O and A'levels, offering both science and arts courses. It is a boarding and day school. The school was founded by the Christian Missionary society, who first arrived in Kaliro in 1904. It became a governmen-aided school in 1982.

The school has seen recent development, including a new gate with perimeter facing, renovated buldings, and the installation of CCTV cameras. WE also have an equiped ICT laboratories and two school buses. The school has achieved notable sports results at the district and regional levels and has also seen record performance in UCE and UACE examinations.

Kaliro High School is also involved in initiatives like Anti-Aids Club, which aims to empower adolescent girls and boys through peer advocacy and awareness. The school also participates in HIV/AIDS awareness programms, including testing for HIV.

We pride ourselves on a legacy of academic excellence, character building, and community engagement. Many of our alumni have gone on to achieve great success in various fields, contributing positively to society both locally and globally. We continue to build upon this strong foundation, preparing the next generation of leaders and responsible citizens.
